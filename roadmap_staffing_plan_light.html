⚡ The staffing principle
Gen-AI agents, MCP and Skills collapse the coding-heavy roles — you need fewer pure developers than a traditional build. What they do not reduce — and what becomes the binding constraint — is the expertise that defines ground truth and earns trust: memory-product QA domain experts, lab / hardware integrators, and the trust-validation owners. So the team is small but senior: fewer, stronger people beat a large junior team when AI does the heavy lifting.

Assumptions & Scaling Levers
This plan assumes:
  • Senior team. AI acceleration rewards experience — these are mid-to-senior engineers, not a large junior pool.
  • Zion lab APIs & historical QA data are reasonably accessible. If lab integration is greenfield or data is messy, add ~1 DevOps/Data engineer in H2.
  • One product line piloted first, then scaled — not all six in parallel during the build.
  • Single co-located/aligned pod. For the ~6-month aggressive variant, run two parallel pods (~18–20 people) — speed comes from parallelism, not a smaller team.
