🔬 From building automation to researching intelligence
With the platform live and trusted, the team shifts from delivering features to advancing capability. This horizon is run as a research portfolio, not a delivery plan: every thrust moves through a stage-gate funnel (Explore → Prototype → Pilot → Adopt) so experiments stay safe of production, and only proven techniques are promoted into Zion. The goal is to evolve Zion from a system that executes testing into one that learns, predicts, and ultimately drives quality autonomously.
How Every Thrust Progresses — the Stage-Gate Funnel
Each research thrust advances through four gates (loosely TRL 2 → 8). A thrust is only promoted when it clears the gate's evidence bar — protecting the production platform from unproven AI.
GATE 1 · TRL 2–3
Explore
Literature, feasibility spikes, data readiness. Question: is it viable on our data?
›
GATE 2 · TRL 4–5
Prototype
Working model on historical data, offline benchmarks vs current baseline.
›
GATE 3 · TRL 6–7
Pilot
Shadow-mode on a live product line; measured against human / current outcomes.
›
GATE 4 · TRL 8
Adopt
Promoted into a Zion component under ModelOps governance & continuous eval.
The Five Research Thrusts
1
RL for Adaptive Testing
REINFORCEMENT
Objective
Stop running fixed suites — an RL agent learns which tests to run, in what order, on which devices to maximize defects-found-per-hour.
Techniques
  • Reward = new defects + coverage gained − time/cost
  • Coverage-guided RL fuzzing for firmware edge cases
  • RL power-cycle / endurance sequencing
  • RL lab scheduling & device allocation
Expected Outcome
Same defect yield in a fraction of the runtime; faster discovery of rare wear/edge failures.
Upgrades

2
Predictive Quality & Reliability
PREDICTION
Objective
Move from anomaly detection to forecasting — predict failures before they happen, in test and in the field. Highest near-term ROI.
Techniques
  • Defect-escape prediction per firmware module
  • NAND endurance / Remaining-Useful-Life models
  • Predictive RMA from field telemetry
  • Predictive release-readiness scoring
Expected Outcome
Fewer field returns; proactive fixes; quantified release risk before sign-off.
Upgrades
Zion.BeaconZion.PulseP7 · P9 · P10
3
Autonomous "Lights-Out" Quality
AUTONOMOUS
Objective
A self-driving quality loop: field anomaly → auto-repro → candidate fix → auto-validate → recommend — with minimal human touch.
Techniques
  • Self-provisioning, self-triaging lights-out lab (+ Robotics)
  • Digital twin of device/lab — test in simulation first
  • Multi-agent orchestration & agentic planning
  • Autonomous RCA + auto-remediation candidates
Expected Outcome
Closed-loop quality that runs nights/weekends unattended; dramatically shorter field-to-fix cycle.
Upgrades
Zion.EnvoyRoboticsZion.FlowEngineP4 · P5 · P10
4
Domain Foundation Models & Causal RCA
FOUNDATION MODEL
Objective
Build memory-product-aware models and causal reasoning so analysis moves beyond correlation to genuine cause.
Techniques
  • Fine-tune / adapters on specs, logs & defect history
  • Knowledge graph + GraphRAG for deep RCA
  • Causal inference (beyond correlation)
  • Generative synthetic workloads (GAN / diffusion)
Expected Outcome
Sharper root-cause, spec-grounded reasoning, richer synthetic test corpora.
Upgrades
Zion.IntelliSpecZion.SentinelZion.VaultP1 · P6
5
Trust, Assurance & ModelOps — the Enabler
ASSURANCE
None of Thrusts 1–4 get adopted without this. It is the backbone that turns research into something a regulator, customer or release board will trust.
Objective
Make every autonomous decision explainable, monitored and reversible.
Techniques
Explainable-AI for decisions · continuous eval & drift monitoring · guardrails · assurance framework for AI-signed releases.
Upgrades
